# ChipHub Writing Rules

Use this skill to produce website articles for `chiphub.top`, not WeChat drafts.

## Output target

The article should feel native to a tech/media information site:
- readable on web and mobile
- suitable for archive, category, tag, and RSS pages
- structured enough for reuse
- opinionated enough to be worth reading

## What good looks like

- Has a clear angle in the opening
- Retains facts from the source
- Adds interpretation instead of paraphrase padding
- Makes the industry or product impact legible
- Explicitly marks uncertainty when facts are incomplete

## What to avoid

- Mechanical summary voice
- Bullet dump replacing narrative
- Fake certainty around rumors
- Overheated self-media tone
- Empty macro statements with no operational meaning

## Good summary/frontmatter behavior

- `title`: do not mechanically copy the source headline; lead with your angle when possible
- `summary`: 1–2 sentences, natural, useful on homepage cards and RSS
- `tags`: prefer a few strong tags over too many weak ones
- `category`: should reflect the main angle, not every possible topic

## Good topic angles for ChipHub

Prefer angles like:
- what changed in supply, infra, model capability, cost, or product strategy
- who is affected next
- whether this matters for builders, operators, model vendors, or buyers
- what is signal versus noise

## Publication stance

Default to `draft: true` unless the user explicitly asks to publish.
Conservative publishing is better than shipping weak posts.
